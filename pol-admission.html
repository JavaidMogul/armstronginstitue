Admissions Policy

SECTION 1

Purpose / Objectives

The purpose of this policy is to provides a clear set of principles to guide Armstrong Institute (AI) in the admission of students to all courses of study, to ensure processes are transparent and decisions are consistent and fair, and that potential students are not disadvantaged because of personal characteristics that are not related to the academic preparation and English language proficiency.

SECTION 2

Accountability

2.1 Delegations and key decision-making power under this Policy

Accountability for the governance of the Institute, i.e., its leadership and decision making, rests with the Corporate Board and its committees, the Academic Board, the Teaching and Learning Committee and the Course Advisory Committee.
Decision making responsibility for administration of admissions rests with the President/CEO or their delegated representative, the Head of Student Administration.

AI Administration is responsible for coordinating and issuing all offers of onshore. admission. Offshore partners are responsible for coordinating and issuing of international offers for offshore admission in which case AI quality standards will apply. Course co-ordinators are responsible for approving the course specific admission criteria and selection methodology to be used to determine which applicants will receive offers of admission. for degrees or research places (which include internships and scholarships). The Institute is. supportive of students under-represented in higher education, from rural or remote locations. or at and educational or social disadvantage.

2.2 Conflicts of interest

All staff involved in selection must disclose where there may be a conflict of interest by completing and submitting a Declaration of Potential or Actual Conflict of Interest Form to Managers of Admissions (Domestic) and AII (International).

The staff member must also withdraw themselves immediately from the situation giving rise to the conflict, pending advice from the manager to whom the disclosure was made.

2.3 Fees

Fees may include tuition fees, non-refundable application fee, enrolment fees, services and equipment fees., Overseas Student Health Cover, textbook and materials fees and any other charges such a reissuance of qualification certificates/statements of attainment (Refer to Armstrong Institute Fees and charges policy).

Students shall pay their fees as stated in their Written Agreement on time and when they are due. Offers of a place is for a full-time course.

A student can pay full fees if the student wishes to, but the student is not required to pay more than 50% of the total tuition fees up front for the course before the student commences the courses that are more than 25 week The Institute can require 100% of the total tuition fees for short course of 25 weeks or less. The steps taken should provider default or student default occur are stated in the Protection of tuition fees paid in advance by student visa holders is undertaken in accordance with the ESOS legislation, the Education Services for overseas Students Act and the Tuition Protection Service (TPS) Framework.

If students delay payment or refuse payment, AI reserves the right to cancel the enrolment. AI is required to inform the Immigration department that a student is no longer enrolled.

2.4 Refunds, Course closure

Details of refunds are available in the Armstrong Institute Fees and Charges Policy. Full refunds are payable if:

▪ AI is unable to provide the academic program offered.
▪ The offer of enrolment is withdrawn for reasons other than incorrect or incomplete information supplied by the student at the time of enrolment.
▪ An Australian “Visa application is refused.

Partial refunds are payable:

▪ When the offer of enrolment is withdrawn because of incorrect or incomplete information supplied by the student at the time of enrolment.
▪ When the student decides not to enrol at least 4 weeks prior to commencement of a course.

No refund is payable if:

▪ AI receives cancellation after commencement date of the student’s course.
▪ After commencing the course, the student withdraws before completing the program.

Requests for refunds must be made in writing. Where a refund is payable, the refund is normally made in Australian dollars, within two weeks of the day on which the student notifies AI inwriting of his/her intention to refuse an offer, or withdraw, and /or becomes eligible for a refund. In such cases the enrolment fee is non-refundable.
In the event of AI defaulting on the course, a full refund is payable within two weeks from the date of notice of the default.
AI must notify TPS via PRISMS (P provider Registration and International Student Management System) of variations in fees, length of courses and qualifications, and details of cases of student default.

SECTION 3

Scope

This policy applies to the admission of applicants to the following awards:

▪ Award coursework study in higher education including domestic and international, onshore and offshore. /dd>
▪ Postgraduate research required in internships or research units.
▪ Non-award units or courses of study including industry-based courses.

SECTION 4

Policy Statement

AI believes in equal access to educational opportunities by people of all backgrounds, experiences, and abilities. In the case of international onshore students, the AI abides by current state and federal government requirements, e.g., assessing an applicant as being a genuine temporary entrant. Students applying to study off-shore by on-line must attend at least one unit offered by on-shore learning.

AI actively seeks to engage with and serve the local community (and will be developing a Community Service IT program).
AI is committed to the principles of admissions transparency as outlined by the Higher Education Higher Education Standards Framework and the Services for Overseas Students Act.

As per the TEQSA Student Retention Policy and Guidelines, AI is committed to supporting the success of all its students and proactively working to retain and enrich their experiences during their studies.

For these reasons, it is AI’s goal, where possible, to be able to offer every applicant a place, whether in the course or program to which they apply, or in another suitable course. This may include the offer of a pathway course or enabling course to address particular learning needs of the student (e.g., an English language or research-intensive courses as a precursor to an award course).

In order to be eligible for selection an applicant must satisfy AI admission criteria, a course’s specific admission criteria, and complete any selection tasks.

Satisfaction of these requirements permits an applicant to be considered for a place but does not guarantee an offer of a place.

4.1 Basis for Admission

Admission offers are made to applicants who are assessed to have the background and abilities to have a reasonable expectation of success in the course they are offered, or substitute course if identified. Applicants must meet the General Educational Requirements for admission and meet two further requirements: detailed in the Admission Procedures, recognition of prior learning (RPL) or work experience and English language proficiency.

All applicants must meet the English language requirements for the level of study for which they are seeking entry. Minimum English language requirements are stated in the Procedure for Admission. Admission offers are made to applicants who are assessed to have the background and abilities to have a reasonable expectation of success in the course they are offered, or substitute course if identified. Applicants must meet the general requirements for admission and meet two further requirements: prior learning and English language proficiency.

4.1.1 Basis for Admission

Admission offers are made on merit based on demonstrated achievement comparable to Australian standards. AI assesses merit and suitability of candidates against our admission criteria for each course, considering one or a combination of:

▪ Academic attainment.
▪ Professional experience and expertise.
▪ Other life experience with a demonstrable relevance to the ﬁeld of study.

AI acknowledges that some people may have circumstances that mean that their ability to demonstrate their capacity via typical means will be limited. AI will consider admission for such people on a case-by-case basis, as detailed in the Admissions Procedure and varied from time to time.
AI uses multiple modes of entry in admitting students, including but not limited to:

▪ Direct applications.
▪ Industry-based group entry to specific courses; and

AI assesses applications based on the following categories:

▪ Group A – Higher Education Study: applicants whose highest level of study enrolment since leaving secondary education is a higher education course.
▪ Group B – Work and Life Experience: applicants who Industry-based group entry to specific courses; andleft secondary education more than two years previously (i.e., who are not classified as recent secondary education applicants) and have not undertaken VET or higher education study since then.
▪ Group C – AI acknowledges the importance of recognising an applicant's prior learning through study or work experience wherever possible and appropriate. AI will provide advanced standing to students who provide evidence of learning in line with AQF Qualifications pathway. Provide clear, transparent, consistent and timely advice to applicants, and communicates decisions in a transparent and timely manner.

In particular, applicants for higher degree by research places will be assessed for suitability against the available pool of expert supervisors to ensure that AI can properly acquit its responsibility to the applicant.

AI will not offer places in specific courses in excess of what the AI reasonably believes it can service.

AI will give specific consideration to the recruitment, admission, participation, and completion of Aboriginal people.

4.2 General requirements for admission

AI requires all applicants for admission to be able to provide evidence of:

1 – Proof of identity
2 – Proof of age
3 – Citizenship status
4 – Visa status if an international onshore Student
5 – The genuineness against specific risk criteria to comply with Streamlined Visa Processing Provider requirements (for international onshore applicants).
6 – English language competency level; and
7 – Educational, employment and other relevant background as detailed in their application.

Details of the methods that are accepted for verification of the items above are provided in the Admissions Procedure as varied from time to time.

AI will adhere to the ESOS legislative framework, including but not limited to the National Code of Practice for Providers of Education and Training to Overseas Students 2018 (National Code) and the Education Services for Overseas Students Act 2000 (ESOS Act) regarding Admissions.

AI will publish the course admission criteria to be applied in each course of study.

Admission criteria and selection methodology for all courses will be applied consistently to all applicants.

Although AI I does not offer admission for higher degrees by research, research components of courses that include research projects or work integrated learning will be as established under the  Research Policy and its associated procedures.

AI will recognise equivalence of admission requirements to individual countries and/or states and will publish these requirements for AI’s major markets online.

The admission criteria will:

a – Be congruent at all times with the requirements of the Australian Qualification and Higher Education Standards Frameworks.
b – Clearly identify any minimum academic criteria, prior knowledge, expertise or experience that is expected of successful applicants prior to commencing study.
c – Not unduly restrict access to courses on grounds that are not demonstrably connected with a person's capacity to successfully undertake the course or AI's capacity to discharge its obligations in delivering it.

Every course must have documented course admission criteria, selection tasks (if applicable) and selection/ranking methodology (where a course is competitive).

Course admission criteria are approved as part of the course approvals process.

Selection methodology and any selection tasks are approved by the Teaching and Learning Committee and the Integrity Committee.

Amendments to course admission criteria, selection tasks (if applicable) may be made by the relevant committee. Changes to admission criteria cannot be made in the middle of an admissions intake for domestic students, or after an admission process has commenced for international students.

All applicants must satisfy the minimum AI education and course admission criteria. Where the course is competitive, applicants may need to satisfy a standard higher than the minimum course admission criteria to be offered a place.

The process by which admission criteria are made and applied is detailed in the Admissions Procedure and may vary from time to time. AI reserves the right to not make an offer to an otherwise qualified applicant in a course which still has available space if that applicant:

(a) – Be congruent at all times with the requirements of the Australian Qualification and Higher Education Standards Frameworks.
(b) – Is currently indebted to AI.
(c) – Has been excluded or had their enrolment cancelled at any other Australian institution.
(d) – Is under 15 years of age; or
(e) – Is deemed not to be a genuine temporary entrant.
